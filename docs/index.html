Child Care project
  • Project Name: SunnyChildCare
  • Description: Hey there! I had the awesome opportunity to build the web structure for SunnyChildCare. It's a platform designed specifically for child care centers, making it super user-friendly for both the staff and parents.
  • Tech & Tools: I got my hands dirty with some cool tools and tech for this one. I used Html5, CSS, and JS for the frontend magic. Webpack helped me bundle everything together, and I managed the code with Git. On the backend, I used Node.js. Oh, and I can't forget Photoshop – it was super handy for some of the design elements.
  • Key Features & Highlights: One of the things I'm super proud of is the responsive design. No matter what device you're on, the site looks great. I also added a feature where customers can shoot over a form straight to a designated email – super convenient. And to make sure everyone knows where the center is, I integrated Google Maps to pinpoint the school's exact location.
  • Challenges & Solutions: Ran into a bit of a hiccup with cross-domain issues. But no worries! With a combo of cors and express, I got that sorted out in no time.
  • Project Link & Code: Curious to see it live? Check it out at www.sunnychildcare.com.
  • Feedback & Results: Since we launched, there's been a 30% uptick in user registrations. Pretty neat, right?
  • What I Learned: One of the coolest things I picked up from this project was the art of color selection, especially in the realm of early childhood education. It's fascinating how different sectors have their own vibe and color palettes.
